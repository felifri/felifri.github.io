About Me

My research interests are centered around artificial intelligence (AI) and how we can make AI systems socially acceptable. I focus on two key directions:

First, I work on incorporating the human in the loop. Inspiration comes from cognitive science, since the exploration and consideration of human behavior are inevitable and possibly the best motivation to improve current models. ChatGPT is just one example where human interaction boosted performance — demonstrating massive improvement through human interaction and the impact on current societies.

Second, I address the fact that current models are heavily biased. Trained on unfiltered web data, they risk reflecting or amplifying societal biases. I am interested in better understanding the learned representations in these models and in how to debias them in arbitrary directions, paving the way for fairer AI. To this end, I want to make use of human capabilities to incorporate knowledge outside of the rigid range of purely data-driven approaches.

Research Topics: Multimodal AI · Generative AI · AI Alignment · AI Safety · Fairness & Bias
